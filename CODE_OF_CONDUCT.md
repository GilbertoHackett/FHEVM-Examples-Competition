# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all participants in the FHEVM Examples project. This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all those who participate in our community, as well as the consequences for unacceptable behavior.

We invite all those who participate in FHEVM Examples to help us create a safe and positive experience for everyone.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants in FHEVM Examples are expected to:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all individuals with respect and kindness, regardless of background, identity, or experience level
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively and professionally
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional standards in all interactions
- **Be Constructive**: Provide feedback that is helpful, specific, and actionable
- **Be Patient**: Understand that people have different experience levels and learning curves
- **Be Open-Minded**: Consider different viewpoints and approaches

### In Code Reviews

- Provide constructive feedback focused on code quality, not on the person
- Ask questions rather than make accusations
- Acknowledge good work and improvements
- Help others learn from feedback
- Be patient with those new to the codebase

### In Communication

- Use inclusive language
- Avoid assumptions about backgrounds or experience
- Be respectful of different time zones and working schedules
- Maintain professionalism in all discussions
- Focus on ideas, not personalities

### In Community

- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Share knowledge and resources generously
- Give credit to others for their contributions
- Support others in their learning journey
- Celebrate successes together

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

### Harassment and Discrimination

- Harassment based on race, color, religion, gender, gender identity or expression, age, national origin, disability, or sexual orientation
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Derogatory comments or jokes
- Intimidation or threats
- Bullying or sustained negative behavior

### Disruptive Behavior

- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks or attacks on others' ideas
- Spamming or off-topic content
- Disrupting discussions or meetings
- Deliberately misleading others

### Violations of Privacy and Security

- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Disclosing security vulnerabilities publicly without responsible disclosure
- Attempting to compromise account security or project integrity
- Violating copyright or intellectual property rights

### Professional Misconduct

- Plagiarism or claiming credit for others' work
- Spreading misinformation
- Posting explicit or offensive content
- Engaging in illegal activities
- Creating a hostile or toxic environment

## Reporting Incidents

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

### How to Report

**Direct Communication** (if safe and comfortable):
- Contact the person privately if you feel comfortable doing so
- Use "I" statements: "I felt uncomfortable when..."
- Assume good intent but address the impact

**To Project Maintainers**:
- Email: [maintainers-email@example.com]
- Subject: "Code of Conduct Concern"
- Include:
  - Description of the incident
  - Date and time (if applicable)
  - People involved
  - Context and impact
  - Any relevant links or references

**Confidential Reporting**:
- All reports will be handled confidentially
- Only necessary parties will be involved in investigation
- Your identity will be protected if requested
- No retaliation will be tolerated

### What Happens After Reporting

1. **Acknowledgment**: You'll receive confirmation that we received your report
2. **Investigation**: We'll investigate the incident promptly
3. **Response**: We'll take appropriate action (see below)
4. **Follow-up**: We'll check in with you about the resolution

---

## Enforcement

We are committed to enforcing this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly.

### Response Process

When a Code of Conduct violation is reported:

1. **Initial Review**: Determine if the report is valid and complete
2. **Investigation**: Gather information from all parties involved
3. **Discussion**: Talk with the involved parties to understand context
4. **Decision**: Determine appropriate action
5. **Resolution**: Implement action and communicate with reporter

### Consequences

Consequences depend on the severity and pattern of behavior:

**Warning**
- Private conversation about expectations
- Clear explanation of violation
- Request for specific behavioral changes
- May be required for first-time minor violations

**Suspension**
- Temporary removal from project activities
- Duration specified (typically 1-30 days)
- May require acknowledgment of conduct before reinstatement
- For repeated violations or serious incidents

**Removal**
- Permanent removal from project
- Loss of commit access
- No participation in future activities
- For severe or unresolved violations

**Escalation**
- Legal action if required
- Report to platform/organization authorities
- For legal violations or safety threats

---

## Special Situations

### Conflicts of Interest

Maintainers with a personal or professional relationship to someone involved in a report will recuse themselves from decision-making.

### Appealing a Decision

If you believe a Code of Conduct decision was unjust:

1. Request a review in writing
2. Provide new information or context
3. A different maintainer will review the appeal
4. Final decision will be provided within 7 days

### Protecting Reporters

- No retaliation against those who report in good faith
- Retaliation itself is a Code of Conduct violation
- Maintain confidentiality of reporters when possible
- Create safe channels for reporting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- Project repositories and code
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Project discussions and forums
- Emails and private messages (when discussing project matters)
- At events representing or associated with the project
- Any spaces managed by project organizers

This Code of Conduct does not apply to:

- Private conversations unrelated to the project
- Spaces managed by other organizations
- Activities conducted outside the project community
